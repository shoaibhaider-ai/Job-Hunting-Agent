========================================
KNOWLEDGE BASE: Job Fair & Career Preparation Slides
Source: University Career Services + AI Industry Talks (April 2026)
========================================

SLIDE 1: What Employers Look For in Junior AI Roles
-----------------------------------------------------
- Strong foundation in Python and data manipulation (pandas, numpy).
- Ability to explain machine learning concepts clearly, not just run libraries.
- Evidence of real projects on GitHub — quality over quantity.
- Communication skills: can you explain technical results to non-technical stakeholders?
- Growth mindset: willingness to learn and adapt quickly.
- Familiarity with the software development lifecycle and version control (Git).

SLIDE 2: Top Skills for 2026 AI/Data Job Market
-------------------------------------------------
- Python remains the #1 language for AI and data science roles.
- Generative AI and prompt engineering are the fastest-growing skill demands.
- SQL is a non-negotiable baseline for data roles.
- Cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP) are increasingly expected even for interns.
- MLOps awareness (Docker, CI/CD, model monitoring) differentiates candidates.
- Business communication and data storytelling are underrated but highly valued.
- Portfolio projects and open-source contributions carry significant weight.

SLIDE 3: How to Stand Out in a Competitive Job Market
-------------------------------------------------------
- Build a portfolio of 3–5 strong GitHub projects with clear README documentation.
- Add a personal website or portfolio page linking all your projects.
- Engage on LinkedIn: post about projects, share learnings, connect with professionals.
- Contribute to open-source projects related to your target role.
- Get certifications from credible platforms: Coursera, DeepLearning.AI, Google.
- Participate in Kaggle competitions to practice applied ML skills.
- Network actively: attend job fairs, webinars, and local tech meetups.

SLIDE 4: Resume Tips for Tech Students
----------------------------------------
- Keep resume to one page for internships and early-career roles.
- Use action verbs to begin every bullet point (Built, Developed, Deployed, Analysed).
- Quantify where possible: "Achieved 93% accuracy", "Reduced processing time by 40%".
- Include GitHub link, LinkedIn, and professional email prominently.
- Tailor resume for each application — highlight skills matching the job description.
- List only tools and languages you can genuinely discuss in an interview.
- Put most relevant experience/projects near the top of the resume.

SLIDE 5: Interview Preparation Strategy
-----------------------------------------
- Study the job description carefully — every keyword is a potential question.
- Prepare concise answers for: "Tell me about yourself" and "Why this company?"
- Practise explaining 2–3 projects end-to-end (problem → approach → result).
- Do at least 3–5 mock technical interviews before the real one.
- Use coding platforms (LeetCode, HackerRank) for algorithm practice.
- Be ready to whiteboard or explain algorithms without an IDE.
- Research the company: products, team, tech stack, recent news.

SLIDE 6: Application Tracking & Follow-Up
-------------------------------------------
- Maintain a spreadsheet or tracker for every application you submit.
- Record: company, role, source, date applied, deadline, interview date, outcome.
- Send a follow-up email 5–7 days after applying if you hear nothing.
- After an interview, always send a thank-you email within 24 hours.
- If rejected, politely ask for feedback — it helps improve future applications.
- Treat each application as a learning experience regardless of outcome.

SLIDE 7: Salary & Negotiation Tips for Interns
------------------------------------------------
- Research market rates before the offer stage (Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary).
- It is acceptable to politely negotiate an internship stipend — most companies expect it.
- If salary is non-negotiable, negotiate other benefits: remote work, mentorship, certificate.
- Never accept or reject an offer on the spot — ask for 24–48 hours to consider.
- Always get the final offer in writing before accepting.

SLIDE 8: Building a Professional GitHub Profile
-------------------------------------------------
- Pin your best 4–6 repositories on your profile page.
- Every repository should have a clear README: what it does, how to run it, sample output.
- Use descriptive commit messages ("Added feature engineering step for model pipeline").
- Keep repositories organised with clear folder structure.
- Add topics/tags to repositories so they appear in GitHub searches.
- Contributions graph: aim for consistent contributions over time.

SLIDE 9: LinkedIn Profile Optimisation
----------------------------------------
- Use a professional headshot and customise your LinkedIn URL.
- Write a compelling headline: "CS Student | Python | ML | Seeking AI Internships".
- Add all relevant skills — recruiters search by skill keywords.
- Request recommendations from professors, project teammates, or supervisors.
- Share posts about your projects, certifications, and learnings regularly.
- Follow companies you want to work for and engage with their content.

SLIDE 10: Emerging AI Trends to Know for Interviews
-----------------------------------------------------
- Large Language Models (GPT-4, Gemini, Claude) are transforming every industry.
- Agentic AI: autonomous agents that plan, reason, and take multi-step actions.
- RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): the dominant pattern for knowledge-grounded AI.
- Fine-tuning vs. prompt engineering: know when to use each approach.
- Multimodal AI: models that process text, image, audio, and video together.
- Responsible AI: bias detection, fairness, transparency, and explainability.
- Edge AI: deploying AI models on low-power devices (mobile, IoT).
